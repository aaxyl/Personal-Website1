Background

My background and interest in IT dates to around 12 years back (around mid-2006), at which time I was given my first computer; a Pentium 4 powered PC. It was at this time when my fascination with IT was sparked, more specifically with computers; what components made up computers and how they work together to compute information. I started tinkering with various laptops and desktops the following years, however it was not until I finished secondary school when I had pieced together my workstation desktop using an old prebuilt computer from 2010, a decommissioned Xeon server CPU and an Nvidia graphics card.

I came to RMIT to study Bachelor of IT, mostly because I've heard RMIT is one of the best schools to study engineering and technology related courses. If I were to be honest, it is also one of the schools that I had sufficient ATAR requirements to get a guaranteed entry into one of my desired courses, the other being network engineering & electrical and electronic engineering.

During my time here at RMIT, I expect to learn more in-depth knowledge about computer systems as well as practical skills (such as programming/scripting) that'll help me with my goal in pursuing a career in system or network management, (i.e: system administration, network engineering).

Ideal Job

Source: https://www.seek.com.au/job/35689341?type=promoted&userqueryid=d3a220a0aa70d262f5549942d73d1c8f-3465635

Network engineering involves the implementation, maintenance, support, development and sometimes the design of communication networks within or between organisations. This job appeals to me most out of the other jobs for two reasons:

It allows me to work with networking equipment out of reach of average tech consumers.

It pays considerably well (range of $60,000 to $120,000+), seeing as one can acquire a position relatively easily with just CCNA (or higher) certification, which can already give me a job with a salary of around $75,000 without prior field experience.

The skills needed for a job of this calibre would require a moderate level of analytical, leadership and organisational skills, as well as attention to detail and the ability to work well in a team. Almost all network engineers would also be expected to have qualifications such as a Bachelor in a computer-related course(i.e: computer science, engineering, programming, IT) and/or Cisco’s CCNA certification. With certain jobs possibly requiring additional certifications (e.g: Juniper, Wireshark, COMPTIA, etc).

Apart from general “people” and work skills (i.e: analytical, organisational, team player skills & attention to detail), I currently do not have the qualifications needed to acquire the position of network engineering. However I am planning to work myself up during my time in University to obtain my Bachelor, perhaps Masters qualification in Information Technology, and Cisco CCNA certification.
